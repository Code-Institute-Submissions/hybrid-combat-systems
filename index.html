About Hybrid Combat Systems

Our system

Based on traditional Japanese teachings, Hybrid Combat Systems has evolved to incorporate modern methods while remaining true to it's histroy. We take traditional Karate movements and augment with modern knowledge to create a robust and powerful system of movement and defense.

Ethos

Hybrid Combat Systems believes in mastering violence so that we can remove the fear of violence from our lives. We strive for excellence and dedication in practice. Practioners are urged to push themselves physically and mentally, to improve themselves and their confidence in all situations.

Culture

We believe in pushing ourselves to stretch the limits of mind and body. However we also encourage rest and recovery for both. We believe in training hard and enjoying downtime. We take a family approach to the club and hope to welcome all newcomers.

Our Instructors

Master Danny Rooney

Founder

Master Rooney is the founder and head of the Irish Shuriken Jutsu Federation. He studied under various masters in Japan in the 1980's before returning to Ireland to found his system. Taking the traditional lessons of Japan and combining them with modern, practical application, Master Rooney was able to create a system as effective as it is beautiful.
Master Rooney rotates between all classes as much as possible and leads all gradings.

Sensai Darren Hayes

Chief Instructor

Sensai Hayes is Chief Instructor of HCS. He has over 30 years experience in martial arts. His background in aikido gives him a strong grounding in the traditional arts. This mixed with the practical modern approach of HCS makes Sensai Hayes a confident and approachable instructor.
Sensai Hayes rotates between all classes as much as possible and leads the Cavan class.

Sensai Stewart Kenny

Sensai Kenny has been practicing the art for over a decade and is highly regarded for his ability and relaxed approach. He has been teaching for over three years and continues to hone his craft. Sensi Kenny has a particular fondness for sword work.
Sensai Kenny rotates between all classes as much as possible and leads the Clondalkan class.

Sensai Hannah Mooney

Sensai Mooney has been practicing the art for over 8 years and teaching for three. She was the first female instuctor and is praised for her skill and determination. Sensai Mooney continues to improve her own practice as well as being generous with her time for any and all students willing to learn.
Sensai Mooney rotates between all classes as much as possible.
